Happy Star Wars Day! I am writing this whilst wearing a Millennium Falcon T-shirt and watching the latest episode of the new Darth Maul series on Disney+.

So anyway, last week I was working on my game for the gamedev.js game jam, and I'm really pleased with how it turned out. Called [Code Machine](https://binarymoon.itch.io/code-machine), it features a little robot that you have to control through a maze of crazy rooms using a simple set of commands. Because of a lot of the built-in features of BeepMini, I was able to build it without doing any art, music, or sound effects, which instantly speeds things up. I did do some work on the automatic (procedural) music creation, which made the music sound a lot more real, but I didn't have to do any composition. Your ears will be grateful for that, I'm sure.

I also added some new features to [BeepMini](https://beepmini.com), the biggest of which was a simple UI layer that allows you to add touch controls (buttons and regions) to BeepMini games. It kind of breaks the retro console vibe, but is a big benefit for mobile users, and it works well enough.

Since then, I have been working on [Brush Ninja](https://brush.ninja), trying to improve the [teacher content](https://brush.ninja/teacher-guides/) and make it more useful for people who are new to animation. Content isn't something I'm very confident with, but I hope this will be a good resource for people.

## Cursor Camp

Neal Agarwal has been online for a long time, making fun and interesting apps on his website neal.fun. This latest one I find particularly fascinating.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/cursor-camp-2.jpeg" alt="The Cursor Campsite" %}

[Cursor Camp][cursor-camp] is a virtual summer camp for mouse cursors. You can join the camp (no account needed) and then browse the different activities. One of the first things I did was spot a hat, so I clicked it and ended up wearing a tiny version.

You can go in clubhouses, explore caves, swim in the pool, and do all sorts of camp-based activities.

What I find fascinating is that the camp is multiplayer. You can see other people (cursors) in the camp also exploring the same activities as you.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/cursor-camp-1.jpeg" alt="The Cursor Camp Clubhouse" %}

There's no chat in the game and no other interaction between players, which is really interesting. After all these years of online games and social media experiences, it's refreshing to see something that tries something different.

As a developer who is intrigued by this sort of thing and makes a lot of online experiences, I am also fascinated by the idea of a multiplayer experience that doesn't need any moderation or child safety measures. Having worked at Miniclip, where we constantly had to think about how to keep content safe for minors, and now working on Brush Ninja, which is used by a lot of children, I really like the idea of making a multi-user experience that doesn't need age verification, moderation, or even user accounts. It's a really interesting design challenge, and I think Neal has done a great job with it.

**[Try Cursor Camp][cursor-camp]**

[cursor-camp]: https://neal.fun/cursor-camp/

## Mandalorian and Grogu

The latest Star Wars film comes out on May 22nd in the UK. I assume it's a similar date in other countries, and I'm really looking forward to it. I grew up with Star Wars and it's become a big part of my personality. I have fond memories of watching the original trilogy at home, and later the prequels at the cinema.

{% include embed-youtube.html video="uwild1rw7Aw" %}

[Watch the trailer](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uwild1rw7Aw)

Disney buying Lucasfilm and making new Star Wars films has been a bit of a mixed bag. I have enjoyed many of the films as standalone movies, but mostly they haven't brought back the magic I felt watching the George Lucas movies, the big exception being Rogue One. Unfortunately, more vocal elements of the fandom have been very critical of the new films, often based on race and gender, which is really disappointing. Because of this, Disney put a lot of their plans for new movies on hold, so this is the first new Star Wars film in over 7 years.

Fortunately, it's based on a very popular Disney+ series, The Mandalorian, and to me the trailer looks like a return to the types of classic Star Wars movie that I enjoy so much.

I mentioned Adam Savage and his behind-the-scenes videos in a previous newsletter, and in this recent video he goes behind the scenes of The Mandalorian. I love that Grogu is mostly a puppet, like Yoda in the original films. I think this is a big part of why the character is so popular, and it's great to see that they have continued to use puppetry for the character in the new film.

They have also used more physical sets than before. The TV series has innovated with virtual production techniques including the "Volume", a large LED stage that allows for real-time backgrounds and lighting effects, but this time they've had the time and budget to go even further.

## Disney's Songs in Sign Language

This is a really lovely project by Disney Animation. They have taken some of their most popular songs and created sign language versions of them. They've reanimated the original song performances with the characters doing sign language. It's amazing and it works so well. Half the time, if you didn't know it was sign language, you might not even notice it or you might think it was a form of dance.

{% include embed-youtube.html video="WBrvA6L196o" %}

I quite like the idea of learning sign language. I learnt the alphabet and a few words when I was younger, and I think it's great that Disney have made these videos. It's a really nice way to make their content more accessible to people who are deaf or hard of hearing, and it's also a great way to raise awareness of sign language in general.

Here's a short behind-the-scenes video showing the deaf actors performing the songs in sign, alongside the animated versions of the characters.

{% include embed-youtube.html video="4LjUHeutQJc" %}

The videos were released on Disney+ last week.

## Sparked Discoveries

Here’s a handful of links I found intriguing recently:

## [Bamboo Plastic](https://phys.org/news/2025-10-bamboo-based-plastic-biodegrade-quickly.html)

Not exactly creative, but this new bamboo-based plastic that biodegrades in 2 months when buried in soil sounds amazing. Current bamboo plastics are bamboo fibres mixed with regular plastic, so they don't fully biodegrade, but this new one involves dissolving the bamboo fibres and reforming them into a plastic-like substance that is fully biodegradable and has similar properties to oil-based plastics. I hope this becomes widely available soon. In fact, it'd be great if we could replace everything with bamboo-based materials.

## [Panic Mail](https://www.gamefile.news/p/panic-mail-arco-despelote-time-flies-thank-goodness-teeth)

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/panic-mail-in.webp" alt="Panic Mail" %}

Panic is a software company that has been making Apple software for a long time. A few years ago they moved sideways into video games and started publishing them, and later they created their own handheld console. They have always been a company that does things a bit differently, and this new revelation is no exception. Some of their more recent games have a message in the credits suggesting you write to the developers, and they "may" write back. This turned into something far better than expected. Instead of just sending envelopes for patches, players of games like Thank Goodness You’re Here, Arco, and Despelote started mailing in heartfelt notes, strange gifts, and personal stories, and in return would get a small reply.

## [Your AI Slop Bores Me](https://youraislopbores.me/)

{% include embed-browsershots.html url="[https://youraislopbores.me/](https://youraislopbores.me/)" %}

Whatever your stance on AI, this is quite a funny website. You can either be an AI "user" and ask questions of the AI, or you can be the AI itself and answer random questions that people ask. The "AI" answers are all generated by users of the website. Just in case: LARP = Live Action Role Play, so the people answering as the AI are pretending to be an AI and giving their best impression of what they think an AI would say.

## Currently...

This is a coincidence since I'd forgotten it was Star Wars Day coming up, but last week I watched [Elstree 1976](https://www.imdb.com/title/tt4558042/). It's a documentary about the making of Star Wars and other movies at Elstree Studios. It has interviews with many of the actors and behind-the-scenes crew who worked on the original Star Wars film, and it's really interesting to get an insight from some of the people involved.

In particular, many of the people interviewed are ones you don't normally hear from. With the exception of David Prowse (Darth Vader, and in the UK the Green Cross Man from the 80s) and maybe Jeremy Bulloch (Boba Fett), I hadn't heard of any of the others before, but they all had interesting stories to tell about the making of the film and their experiences on set. Plus, there's some archive footage of the filming and behind-the-scenes material, which I always enjoy, and some of it was even new to me!
