Hope you're doing well. I recently got back from a trip to London. I am from South East London originally but now live in the countryside, so it's always nice to go back and see the city, and also to catch up with family.

While in London we went to the Natural History Museum to see the dinosaurs, and more importantly (for my son) the Pokemon collaboration. It was a lot of fun and the museum is always amazing.

This newsletter is going to be a little shorter than normal because I am in the middle of a gamejam and having been on holiday I missed half the time I would have had. I'll talk about the jam in the minute, but first I wanted to share some of the things we did on while we were away!

## Pixel Cam

Shortly before we left for London I made a [small camera tool][pixelcam-link]. I thought it would be fun to make a retro pixel art camera inspired by the Gameboy Camera. I've seen a few people posting images taken with Gameboy Cameras recently and since I have some experience with photo apps I thought it would be fairly easy to do. I ended up doing some work on it while we were away but it was fun using it to take random photos around the city.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/brush-ninja-photo-00001.png" alt="Rochester Castle" %}

My son and I had a lot of fun picking out colour palettes to use and then using them to take photos whilegoing around London. I actually broke the app one morning and didn't realise until we had left the hotel and were on our way into central London. Fortunately my web host has a control panel that let me revert to an earlier version so I was able to fix it standing on the platform at the train station.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/brush-ninja-photo-00002.png" alt="The Natural History Museum gardens" %}

One of the things I am most pleased with is that I was able to turn it into a PWA so you can [install it on your phone](https://brush.ninja/camera/) and use it like a normal camera app, including when not offline. As long as you "install it" (or add it to your home screen) then it will work almost the same as a normal camera app.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/brush-ninja-photo-00003.png" alt="The Natural History Museum front entrance" %}

I have plans to turn this into a video camera that will create short animated GIFs in this pixelly style but that will take a bit longer to finish off. In the meantime you can try the camera out for yourself, and I'd love to see any photos you take with it!

## My Neighbour Totoro

For me one of the highlights of the trip was getting to see My Neighbour Totoro in the West End. I have been a huge fan of Studio Ghibli for a long time, and I have seen many of their films multiple times, but I had never seen any of them live on stage before. It was a really fun experience and the show was magical. The Totoro puppets were amazing, the set design fantastic, and it was surprisingly funny.

It turns out the puppets were made by the Jim Henson company which I thought was awesome. If you get a chance to go and see it then I highly recommend it.

## Aardman at the Young V&A

If you read this because of [Brush Ninja](https://brush.ninja) then you know I am very interested in animation. At one stage I wanted to work for Pixar or Disney but I ended up working in web dev and games instead. But I still love animation and find the process and craft fascinating.

As such this exhibition at the Young V&A was a treat. I've actually seen an Aardman exhibition before in Bristol where Aardman are based, but this one had all sorts of new models and drawings so it was great getting up close to the real thing.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/aardman-00007.jpeg" alt="How to animate a walk cycle" %}

The models in particular were, as expected, amazing. They had examples of Robin from Robin Robin, Wallace and Gromit, Shaun the Sheep - and Over the Garden Wall which I wasn't even aware Aardman had worked on.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/aardman-00008.jpeg" alt="A set from the Wrong Trousers with a sleeping Wallace stealing a jewel from the museum" %}

Besides the models I really enjoy seeing the sketches and storyboards that show the process of creation. I love seeing the evolution of ideas and how everything comes together in the final product.

Below are a few of the dozens of photos I took of the exhibition. If you get a chance to see it then I highly recommend it.
