## Resolutions

I don't make traditional new years resolutions but I do like to set goals for the year. One of the things I would like to do this year is to get better at artistic things. I have always been interested in art, and studied it at school, but I don't feel like I have improved much since then. So this year I want to spend time trying to get better at drawing/ painting, and 3D art.

### Drawing and Painting

{% include embed-youtube.html video="9ceNUTFVcsM" %}

I tend to draw unrealistic things. Cartoons and fantasy stuff is most fun for me. I have a few sketch books on the go at all times and I would like to get better at drawing people, and environments/ landscapes. Especially from imagination.

I have a bunch of how to draw books from the [Etherington Brothers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Etherington_Brothers), 2 prolific comic artists from the UK. They publish loads of [tutorial images on social media](https://www.instagram.com/etheringtonbrothers/), and then compile them into books, selling a new book each year (via kickstarter). I plan to work through some of these books and practice the techniques they teach.

I also want to do more life drawing. But I don't have much free time so I am going to use one of the many websites that provide life drawing references. _Keep in mind, if you're clicking through to these sites that they do feature non-sexual nudity_. I have used [Line of Action](https://line-of-action.com/), [Sketch Daily](https://www.sketchdaily.net/en), and [Quick Poses](https://quickposes.com/), and they all have something different to offer. Worth checking out for timed drawing practice.

I also like watching videos of artists drawing and painting on YouTube. Some of my favourites are:

* [Stephen Travers Art](https://www.youtube.com/@stephentraversart/shorts) - Wonderful pen and ink drawings with lots of tips and techniques.
* [James Gurney](https://www.youtube.com/user/gurneyjourney) - James is a brilliant traditional artist, and author of the _Dinotopia_ books. He has a lot of great videos about painting techniques and materials.
* [Proko](https://www.youtube.com/user/ProkoTV) - Proko has a lot of great videos about figure drawing and anatomy. His artistic style is very oriented towards manga comic books but the techniques are applicable to all styles.
* [Vanidas Mangathil](https://www.youtube.com/@VanidasMangathilArt/videos) - Vanidas is a self taught watercolour artist with a lovely loose style. His videos are like ASMR - very relaxing to watch. But also I find them inspirational for the amount of detail he can get from such few brush strokes.

### 3D Art

{% include embed-youtube.html video="BzTN2yTgTj0" %}

I studied 3D art with 3D Studio Max at university because I wanted to work in video games as an artist. But that never happened. During Covid I started learning [Blender]({% link _library/app/2025-03-20-blender.md %}) which I really enjoyed using but I never had an end goal in mind. This year however I want to make some simple 3D games so it might be time to get back into Blender and learn some more advanced techniques.

For getting started I am going to work through Grant Abbits tutorials on [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/c/GrantAbbitt). He has a lot of great beginner and intermediate tutorials that cover a wide range of topics. Sice it's been a while I will start with his [Blender 5 beginers guide](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLn3ukorJv4vv9_e-htADGsPX9TMaQpHV8) - hopefully I can speedrun it since I already know the basics. Then I will look at his [animation tutorials](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLn3ukorJv4vuJVY3cjDJyt9bK6D-xACO3) since I want to make some simple animated characters for my games.

Other people/ tutorials that look good include:

* Lacruzo - [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/@lacruzo/videos) only a few tutorials but they have a lovely cartoony style that I am a fan of. The tutorials cover grease pencil (2D in 3D) and low poly modelling, all in a cel-shaded style.
* Polygon Runway - [YouTube channel](https://www.youtube.com/c/PolygonRunway) and [website](https://polygonrunway.com/) - This is where I started during Covid. The paid tutorials are great and start very slowly taking you through the basics (start with the Become a 3D illustrator course). The later paid tutorials get going a lot quicker and assume a higher level of knowledge.
* Crashsune Academy - [YouTube playlist](https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLcaQc6eQjXCzYski4EEr-iRfdkXkX-P3g) - This is a playlist of tutorials focused on making a low polygon anime cartoon character. Looks like a good fit for what I want to do.

{% include embed-image.html image="/images/updates/2026/enclose-horse.png" %}

The larger the paddock the more points you get, but there's limited fence pieces and some obstacles (like portals?) that make it more challenging. There's a new puzzle each day.
